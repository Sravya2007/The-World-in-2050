The year is 2050.

How much progress has science and technology made in the past 3 decades?

1. Hyperloops

Hyperloops are the modern transportation systems. They can propel passengers or cargo at speeds of over 1000 km/h. The Hyperloop system consists of sealed and partially evacuated tubes, connecting mobility hubs in large metropolitan areas, and pressurized vehicles, usually called pods, which can move at very high speeds, thanks to contactless levitation and propulsion systems as well as to the low aerodynamic drag.

  • This technology transports people in lesser time compared to traditional modes of transport.
  • It consumes less energy and is a sustainable mode of transport.
  • It is immune to bad weather conditions and is earthquake resistant.
  • US: Chicago to Columbus to Pittsburgh, Dallas to Laredo to Houston, Cheyenne to Denver to Pueblo, Miami to Orlando
  • Canada: Toronto to Ottawa to Montreal
  • Mexico: Mexico City to Guadalajara
  • United Kingdom: Edinburgh to London, Glasgow to Liverpool
  • India: Bengaluru to Chennai, Mumbai to Chennai, Pune to Mumbai

2. Electric vehicles

Since the 2030s, electric cars have gained momentum and are widely used. In fact, as of 2050, there are very few petrol or diesel-powered vehicles on the road today. These cars are very attractive and have taken over the world thanks to innovative, pollution-free and improved technology.

  • Better battery range and longer durability. Batteries can be recycled too.
  • In the past few years, self-driving technology has improved but it is yet to be developed further.
  • Sustainability. These cars are energy-efficient and cause much less carbon emissions than older cars.
  • Electric cars can get charged within minutes, thanks to the technology that has been improved over the years.
  • Production technology has made electric vehicles available for the masses by ensuring that supply keeps up with rising demand.
  • Infrastructure tech has made fast charging convenient for everyone, regardless of whether you're at home or on the road.
  • Smartphone apps have made it easier to find nearby charging stations.

3. IoT

As of today, IoT technology is in 95% of electronics for new product designs. Nowadays, every device or appliance is connected to the cloud and internet. Over the past three decades, our world has been turned upside down by IoT devices and connectivity.

  • Home Comforts: Smart homes, all rooms filled with sensors which track movements of owners and react accordingly.
  • Smart Offices: Improved security through face recognition, coworking spaces and better collaboration.
  • Health: Health sensors, monitor vital stats, recommend diets, order prescriptions and call medical attention, surgery by robots.
  • Sensors: They collect data from their environment. Multiple sensors can be bundled together or can be part of a device.
  • Connectivity: Sensors are connected to the cloud through cellular, satellite, WiFi, Bluetooth and so on.
  • Data Processing: The cloud software processes the data and checks some conditions according to the use case.
  • User Interface: This information is made useful to the user through apps or web browsers.

4. Habitable Mars

A permanent team of scientists and robotics engineers is present on Mars. SpaceX is leading the effort to make Mars habitable. The first civilian tourists have also arrived on Mars.

  • The Mars base will soon be expanded with additional facilities providing more energy, food production, recycling systems, mining equipment, and other tools.
  • Satellites containing solar panels orbit Mars provide energy to the Mars base.
  • Vehicles are supplied with hydrogen-based fuel as well, enabling scientists to roam hundreds of kilometers.
  • The habitat modules are constructed partially underground, protecting humans from the harmful ultraviolet rays of the Sun.
  • Radiation-absorbing materials based on nanotechnology are also used in spacesuits and the exterior of vehicles, providing long-term protection against the environment outside.
  • Valuable metals are being mined on Mars and there is ongoing mining of asteroids in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.
  • The mineral wealth in these asteroids is equivalent to about $100 billion per individual.

5. 3D Printing

Over the past decade, the entire 3D printing industry, from hardware and software to materials, has become ready for production. 3D printing technology is a very popular manufacturing process, and a large number of smart 3D printing factories have been setup.

  • Clothing: A 3D printer with sufficient resolution and material capability can print various articles of clothing.
  • Firearms: 3D printed firearms can be downloaded and printed easily, by anyone who has a 3D printer.
  • Health: 3D printers are widely used in the health sector for printing organs to be transplanted, thus saving the lives of many.
  • Modeling: Manufacturing companies typically design object models using a special type of computer software computer-aided design (CAD).
  • Printing: Most 3D printers build objects by depositing layers of material onto a bed. The 3D printer will build the object layer-by-layer. Thermoplastic pellets or beads are extruded out of the printer head and fall onto the bed where they form the printed object.
  • Finishing: Final touches on the printed object include adding solvents to eliminate any imperfections and also creating a smoother surface finish.
